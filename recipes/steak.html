Steak Recipe

Home

Description

Here is how to cook steak like a chef, pan seared and basted with garlic thyme butter! It is dramatic, simple cooking at its best, you will look like a total pro and feel like you are dining at the best steakhouse in town...

Ingredients

  • 1/2 boneless ribeye or scotch fillet
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • 75g / 5 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme or 3 sprigs rosemary
  • 5 garlic cloves

Steps

  1. Bring to Room Temp: Take the steak out of the fridge 30 minutes prior to bring to room temperature.
  2. Dry Steaks: Pat dry with paper towels.
  3. Heat Skillet: Heat oil in a heavy based skillet over high heat until it is very hot, you should see smoke!
  4. Season:Sprinkle each side of the steak generously with salt and pepper, then immediately place in the skillet.
  5. Sear Side 1:Cook for 2/2.5 minutes until you get a great crust, then turn.
  6. Garlic Butter:Leave for 1 minute, then push steaks to one side and toss in butter, garlic and thyme.
  7. Baste:As soon as the butter is melted, continuously spoon the butter over the steak until it is cooked to your liking, 2 minutes in total for the 2nd side for medium rare (52C/125F, chart below for other doneness temps)
  8. Basting also renders fat on the side of the steak, use tongs to sear the edges at the end if you want it browned more.
  9. Rest:Transfer steak to a plate and cover loosely with foil, rest for 5 to 10 minutes.
  10. Servesteak with a bit of the butter from the skillet drizzled on top. Pictured in post with Crispy Smashed Potatoes.
